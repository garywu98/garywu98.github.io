About Me

I go by Gary, but to anyone who’s made it through a late-night debugging session or a muddy camping trip with me, I’m just G.

I’m a software engineer with about 3 years of experience working on scalable backend systems, distributed architectures, and real-time data pipelines. I’ve led Agile teams as a Scrum Master, guiding projects through the full development lifecycle from architecture and planning to production deployment. I enjoy taking ownership, fostering collaboration, and helping teams move quickly and effectively while building high-quality solutions.

Currently, I’m pursuing a Master’s in Computer Science to deepen my expertise in large-scale systems, databases, and AI-driven applications. This academic path complements my professional experience and sharpens my ability to solve complex, real-world problems through a research-informed lens.

Outside of work and school, I’ve played football for over a decade and while I’ve retired my cleats (for now), the obsession lives on. Saturdays and Sundays are sacred. You’ll find me on the couch, emotionally invested in every down like I’m auditioning for a coordinator role. I still love breaking down schemes, arguing about coverages, and pretending I could’ve made that tackle (I couldn’t).

When I’m not watching football, I’m probably out golfing, hiking, camping, or chasing the perfect shot on my Fujifilm X-T30 II, usually while trying not to drop it off a cliff. I hit the gym to balance out my snack intake during game days and catch live music whenever I can because nothing reboots the brain like a wall of sound and overpriced venue nachos.
